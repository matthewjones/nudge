What is Nudge?

Nudge is a simple budgeting tool built to help you see where your money actually goes. No sign-ups, no ads — your data stays right here in your browser.

Whether you're getting an allowance, working a part-time job, or saving up for something big, Nudge helps you track what comes in, what goes out, and what's left.

Why Should I Budget?

Here's the thing — budgeting isn't about being cheap. It's about being intentional. When you know where your money is going, you can make better choices with it.

A few real-world reasons:

  • You stop the "where did my money go?" feeling. Ever check your balance and wonder how it got so low? Tracking fixes that.
  • Subscriptions add up fast. That $5/month here and $10/month there? That's $180/year before you've even bought anything.
  • Saving becomes way easier. When you see how much you spend on eating out vs. groceries, you'll spot easy wins.
  • You build a skill for life. Seriously — adults who budget have less stress about money. Starting now puts you way ahead.

How to Use Nudge

1. Add Your Income 💵

On the Budget page, add anything that puts money in your pocket:

  • Allowance
  • Part-time or freelance work
  • Birthday money
  • Side hustle earnings

Just type the source, enter the amount, and hit + Add.

2. Log Your Expenses 💸

Every time you spend money, log it. Pick the closest category:

| Category | What Goes Here | |----------|---------------| | 🛒 Groceries | Snacks from the store, ingredients for cooking | | 🍕 Eating Out | Fast food, coffee, lunch with friends | | 🎮 Fun | Movies, games, concerts, hanging out | | 👕 Clothes | Outfits, shoes, accessories | | 📱 Tech | Apps, gadgets, accessories | | 🔄 Subscriptions | Spotify, Netflix, iCloud, Game Pass, gym | | 🚌 Transport | Gas money, bus pass, Uber rides | | 📚 School | Supplies, books, club fees | | 💰 Savings | Money you're setting aside on purpose | | 🎁 Other | Gifts, donations, random stuff |

3. Check Your Balance

The three tiles at the top update automatically:

  • Income — total money in
  • Spent — total money out
  • Balance — what's left (goes red if you're overspending)

4. View Your Report 📊

Head to the View page for a full breakdown:

  • See a bar chart of where your money went
  • Check your top 5 biggest expenses
  • Get a status check: 🟢 good, 🟡 getting close, 🔴 over budget

5. Navigate By Month

Use the ◀ ▶ arrows to look at past or future months. Each month has its own separate budget.

6. Back Up Your Data

Your data lives in this browser only. If you clear your browser data, it's gone. Use the Export Backup button to save a copy, and Import Backup to restore it anytime.

Quick Budgeting Tips

  • Pay yourself first. Before spending anything, move some money into Savings. Even $5-10 counts.
  • The 50/30/20 idea. A popular rule of thumb: ~50% on needs (groceries, transport, school), ~30% on wants (fun, eating out, clothes), ~20% into savings. Adjust it to fit your life.
  • Track everything for one month. You'll be surprised what you find. Most people have at least one category that's way higher than they expected.
  • Review your subscriptions. Open the View page and look at the 🔄 Subscriptions bar. If it's bigger than you thought, cancel something you're not using.
  • It's not about being perfect. Going over budget one month doesn't mean you failed. It means you learned something. Adjust and keep going.
